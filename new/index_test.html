Impact of Climate Change on Storm Frequency

Historical Storm Patterns (1950-2000)

Analysis of historical storm data reveals baseline patterns of severe weather events across the United States before significant climate change acceleration.

Recent Trends (2000-2020)

The past two decades show a marked increase in storm frequency and intensity, particularly in tornado activity and severe thunderstorms.

Regional Impact Variations

Different regions experience varying impacts: Southeast sees increased hurricanes, Midwest faces more tornadoes, West Coast has longer wildfire seasons.

Future Projections (2020-2050)

Climate models project continued increases in extreme weather events, with emphasis on heat waves, intense precipitation, and stronger hurricanes.
